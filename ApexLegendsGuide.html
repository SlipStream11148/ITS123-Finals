Getting Started

Welcome to the Apex Games Legends!

The Apex Games are a high-stakes battle royale where only the strongest, smartest, and most adaptable competitors survive. Whether you're a seasoned veteran or a fresh recruit, mastering Apex Legends requires a mix of strategy, skill, and teamwork. Here's how you can elevate your game and dominate the arena:

Legends

Each Legend in Apex has unique abilities that can turn the tide of battle. Experiment with different characters to find one that suits your playstyle:

  • Assault Legends are great for aggressive players who like to push fights.
  • Support Legends excel at keeping the team alive and providing utility.
  • Recon Legends are perfect for gathering intel and tracking enemies.
  • Control Legends are ideal for locking down areas and controlling the flow of combat.

Assault Legends

Bangalore

The professional soldier, Bangalore uses smoke and airstrikes to control the battlefield. Ideal for players who like to play tactically and create chaos.

  • Passive: Double Time - Taking fire while sprinting grants a brief speed boost.
  • Tactical: Smoke Launcher - Fire a high-velocity smoke canister that explodes into a smoke wall on impact.
  • Ultimate: Rolling Thunder - Call in an artillery strike that slowly rolls across the battlefield, dealing damage and slowing enemies.

Fuse

The explosives expert, Fuse uses grenades and his Motherlode to bombard enemies from a distance. Great for players who love dealing explosive damage and creating chaos.

  • Passive: Grenadier - Carry extra grenades and throw them farther, faster, and more accurately.
  • Tactical: Knuckle Cluster - Launch a cluster bomb that continuously expels airburst explosives on impact.
  • Ultimate: The Motherlode - Launch a bombardment that encircles a target area in a wall of flame.

Ash

The ruthless hunter, Ash uses her snare and portals to trap and outmaneuver enemies. Great for players who like to control enemy movement and flank aggressively.

  • Passive: Marked for Death - See death boxes on the map and mark surviving attackers. Gains a new feature of Multi-directional Dash.
  • Tactical: Arc Snare - Throw a spinning snare that damages and tethers the first enemy who gets too close.
  • Ultimate: Phase Breach - Tear opens a one-way portal to a targeted location.

Mad Maggie

The rebellious warlord, Maggie uses her wrecking ball and riot drill to break through defenses and deal massive damage. Perfect for players who love aggressive, in-your-face playstyles.

  • Passive: Warlord’s Ire - Temporarily highlight enemies you’ve damaged and move faster with shotguns.
  • Tactical: Riot Drill - Fire a drill that burns through obstacles and damages enemies on the other side.
  • Ultimate: Wrecking Ball - Throw a ball that releases speed-boosting pads and detonates near enemies.

Ballistic

The weapon master, Ballistic uses his Smart Bullet and Weapon Enhancer to punish enemies and empower his team. Great for players who love aggressive, weapon-focused playstyles.

  • Passive: Sling - Carry a third weapon in your Sling slot (cannot be upgraded with attachments). This weapon can be quickly swapped to but cannot be reloaded manually.
  • Tactical: Smart Bullet - Fire a projectile that locks onto an enemy. If the enemy fires their weapon while locked, their gun will overheat, causing damage over time.
  • Ultimate: Weapon Enhancer - Deploy a field that enhances your team’s weapons: faster reloads, infinite ammo, and increased handling. Ballistic’s Sling weapon is upgraded to gold-tier during this time.

Skirmisher Legends

Pathfinder

The friendly robot, Pathfinder uses his grappling hook and ziplines to outmaneuver enemies. Great for players who love mobility and verticality.

  • Passive: Insider Knowledge - Scan survey beacons to reveal the next ring location.
  • Tactical: Grappling Hook - Grapple to quickly reach out-of-reach places or escape danger.
  • Ultimate: Zipline Gun - Create a zipline for everyone to use, allowing for quick rotations or escapes.

Wraith

The interdimensional skirmisher, Wraith uses her void abilities to reposition and escape danger. Perfect for aggressive players who like to outplay their opponents.

  • Passive: Voices from the Void - Hear a voice warning you when danger is near (e.g., enemies aiming at you).
  • Tactical: Into the Void - Enter the void to reposition quickly, avoiding all damage.
  • Ultimate: Dimensional Rift - Create two portals for 60 seconds, allowing your team to travel between them instantly.

Octane

The adrenaline junkie, Octane uses stims and jump pads to move faster than anyone else. Ideal for players who love speed and aggressive plays.

  • Passive: Swift Mend - Automatically regenerate health over time.
  • Tactical: Stim - Move 30% faster for 6 seconds at the cost of health.
  • Ultimate: Launch Pad - Deploy a jump pad that catapults players into the air.

Revenant

The synthetic nightmare, Revenant uses silence and death totems to disrupt enemies and cheat death. Ideal for players who like to play aggressively and punish ability-heavy teams.

  • Passive: Assassin’s Instinct - Crouch-walk faster and climb walls higher than other Legends.
  • Tactical: Shadow Pounce - Unleash a powerful pounce forwards. Hold to charge for farther distances.
  • Ultimate: Forged Shadows - Forge a shroud of hardened shadows around you that block 50 damage and regenerate for 25 seconds. Knockdowns will start regenerating your shadows.

Horizon

The gravitational manipulator, Horizon uses her gravity lift and black hole to control the battlefield. Ideal for players who love mobility and area control.

  • Passive: Spacewalk - Improved air control and reduced fall impact.
  • Tactical: Gravity Lift - Reverse the flow of gravity, lifting players upward.
  • Ultimate: Black Hole - Deploy a device that creates a micro black hole, pulling in nearby players.

Alter

The reality bender, Alter uses her portals and rifts to manipulate the battlefield and outmaneuver enemies. Perfect for players who love strategic, control-focused playstyles.

  • Passive: Dimensional Shift - After taking damage, Alter can briefly phase into another dimension, becoming invulnerable and moving faster for a short duration.
  • Tactical: Void Rift - Create a portal that teleports allies and enemies to a targeted location. Enemies are disoriented upon arrival.
  • Ultimate: Reality Tear - Tears open a massive rift in the battlefield that slows and damages enemies who pass through it. Allies can use the rift to reposition quickly.

Recon Legends

Bloodhound

The technological tracker, Bloodhound uses their skills to hunt down enemies and reveal their positions. Perfect for players who love gathering intel and leading the charge.

  • Passive: Tracker - See tracks left behind by enemies, such as footprints, and how long ago they were made.
  • Tactical: Eye of the Allfather - Briefly reveal enemies, traps, and clues through walls in front of you.
  • Ultimate: Beast of the Hunt - Transform into the ultimate hunter, enhancing your senses to see cold tracks and move faster. Your vision turns grayscale, but enemies are highlighted in red.

Crypto

The surveillance expert, Crypto uses his drone to scout enemies and EMP their shields. Perfect for players who like to gather intel and support their team from a distance.

  • Passive: Neurolink - Enemies detected by Crypto’s drone are visible to his team.
  • Tactical: Surveillance Drone - Deploy an aerial drone that can scout and interact with the environment.
  • Ultimate: Drone EMP - Detonate an EMP blast from the drone, damaging shields and slowing enemies.

Valkyrie

The skyward warrior, Valkyrie uses her jetpack and missiles to dominate from above. Perfect for players who love verticality and repositioning.

  • Passive: VTOL Jets - Use your jetpack to reposition or gain high ground.
  • Tactical: Missile Swarm - Fire a swarm of mini-rockets that damage and disorient enemies.
  • Ultimate: Skyward Dive - Launch into the air and skydive to reposition across the map.

Seer

The ambush artist, Seer uses his heartbeats and micro-drones to track and disrupt enemies. Ideal for players who like to gather intel and control enemy movements.

  • Passive: Heart Seeker - Hear and visualize the heartbeats of enemies when aiming down sights.
  • Tactical: Focus of Attention - Send out micro-drones to interrupt and reveal enemies.
  • Ultimate: Exhibit - Create a sphere of micro-drones that reveal enemies moving quickly or firing their weapons.

Vantage

The sniper specialist, Vantage uses her bat companion and custom sniper to mark and eliminate enemies from a distance. Great for players who love long-range combat and providing intel.

  • Passive: Spotter’s Lens - Use your sniper scope to reveal enemy information (shield type, range, and squad size).
  • Tactical: Echo Relocation - Launch into the air using your bat companion, Echo.
  • Ultimate: Sniper’s Mark - Fire a custom sniper shot that marks enemies for increased damage.

Support Legends

Gibraltar

The shielded fortress, Gibraltar is a defensive powerhouse who protects his team with shields and airstrikes. Ideal for players who like to anchor their squad and provide cover.

  • Passive: Gun Shield - Aiming down sights deploys a gun shield that blocks incoming damage.
  • Tactical: Dome of Protection - Throw down a dome shield that blocks attacks for 12 seconds. Heals and revives are faster inside the dome.
  • Ultimate: Defensive Bombardment - Call in a concentrated mortar strike to bombard a marked area.

Lifeline

The combat medic, Lifeline keeps her team alive with healing drones and Halo Shield.

  • Passive: Combat Glide - Deploy D.O.C. to revive teammates automatically, leaving your hands free to fight or defend.
  • Tactical: D.O.C. Heal Drone - Deploy a drone that heals nearby teammates over time.
  • Ultimate: D.O.C Halo - Throw D.O.C to activate his Halo Shield System. All players inside use Health and Shield consumables faster.

Mirage

The holographic trickster, Mirage uses decoys to confuse and outwit his enemies. Perfect for players who love mind games and bamboozling opponents.

  • Passive: Now You See Me… - Automatically cloak when using a respawn beacon or reviving a teammate.
  • Tactical: Psyche Out - Send out a holographic decoy to confuse enemies.
  • Ultimate: Life of the Party - Deploy a team of decoys that mimic your movements, making it hard for enemies to track you.

Loba

The high-class thief, Loba uses her bracelet to teleport and her Black Market to loot quickly. Great for players who want to gear up fast and support their team with high-tier loot.

  • Passive: Eye for Quality - See epic and legendary loot through walls.
  • Tactical: Burglar’s Best Friend - Throw a bracelet to teleport to a nearby location.
  • Ultimate: Black Market Boutique - Place a device that lets you and your teammates teleport nearby loot to your inventory.

Newcastle

The heroic defender, Newcastle uses his shields and castle walls to protect his team and revive allies safely. Ideal for players who like to play defensively and support their squad.

  • Passive: Retrieve the Wounded - Drag downed allies while reviving them and deploy a shield for protection.
  • Tactical: Mobile Shield - Deploy a controllable energy shield that moves with you.
  • Ultimate: Castle Wall - Leap to an ally or target area and create a fortified stronghold.

Conduit

The shield specialist, Conduit uses her energy pulses and barricades to protect and heal her team. Ideal for players who like to support their squad with shields and defensive utility.

  • Passive: Savior’s Speed - Gain a temporary speed boost when running toward teammates outside a certain range.
  • Tactical: Radiant Transfer - Send out a pulse of energy that restores shields to teammates.
  • Ultimate: Energy Barricade - Deploy a barrier that damages enemies and recharges ally shields over time.

Controller Legends

Caustic

The toxic trapper, Caustic uses Nox gas to lock down areas and punish enemies who push recklessly. Great for defensive players who like to control the flow of combat.

  • Ultimate: Nox Gas Grenade - Blanket a large area in Nox gas, damaging and slowing enemies.

Wattson

The static defender, Wattson uses electric fences and interception pylons to lock down areas and protect her team. Great for players who like to play defensively and control the battlefield.

  • Passive: Spark of Genius - Ultimate Accelerants fully charge your ultimate, and you can carry more of them.
  • Tactical: Perimeter Security - Place electric fences that damage and slow enemies who pass through them.
  • Ultimate: Interception Pylon - Place a pylon that destroys incoming ordnance and repairs shields.

Rampart

The modder, Rampart uses her walls and minigun to lock down areas and deal massive damage. Perfect for players who like to set up defensive positions and shred enemies.

  • Passive: Modded Loader - Increased magazine capacity and faster reloads with LMGs and the Sheila minigun.
  • Tactical: Amped Cover - Build a crouch-cover wall that blocks incoming shots and amps outgoing damage.
  • Ultimate: Mobile Minigun “Sheila” - Equipped with Mobile Minigun or can be placed into mounted minigun that anyone can use.

Catalyst

The ferrofluid master, Catalyst uses her spikes and dark veil to control the battlefield and lock down areas. Perfect for players who like to play defensively and manipulate enemy movement.

  • Passive: Barricade - Reinforce doors to strengthen them and lock out enemies.
  • Tactical: Piercing Spikes - Throw out a patch of ferrofluid that turns into spikes when enemies are near.
  • Ultimate: Dark Veil - Raise a permeable wall of ferrofluid that slows and blinds enemies who pass through.

Weapons

Select your best or comfortable weapon and its strategy to when to use it that varies depending on the map rotation.

Assault Rifles (AR)

Assault rifles are versatile weapons that excel at mid-range combat, offering a balance of damage, accuracy, and fire rate.

  • R-301 Carbine - A reliable, easy-to-control rifle with great accuracy. Ideal for mid-range engagements.
  • Hemlok Burst AR - A burst-fire rifle that can also switch to single-fire mode. Great for mid-to-long range.
  • VK-47 Flatline - A hard-hitting rifle with moderate recoil. Good for close-to-mid range.
  • HAVOC Rifle - An energy rifle with a wind-up time but high DPS. Best for close-to-mid range with proper timing.
  • Nemesis Burst AR - A burst-fire energy rifle that ramps up fire rate with sustained fire. Great for mid-range pressure.

Submachine Guns (SMG)

SMGs are perfect for close-range combat, offering high fire rates and mobility.

  • R-99 - A fast-firing SMG with high DPS. Great for close-range shredding.
  • Volt SMG - An energy SMG with consistent damage and low recoil. Ideal for close-to-mid range.
  • CAR SMG - A hybrid SMG that can use both Light and Heavy ammo. Great for close-range flexibility.
  • Prowler Burst PDW - A burst-fire SMG with high damage per burst. Good for close-range burst damage.

Light Machine Guns (LMG)

LMGs are high-capacity weapons designed for sustained fire and suppressing enemies.

  • Spitfire - A heavy ammo LMG with a large magazine. Good for mid-range suppression.
  • L-STAR - An energy LMG that overheats instead of reloading. Great for aggressive sustained fire.
  • Devotion LMG - An energy LMG with a wind-up time but high DPS. Best for close-to-mid range with proper timing.

Sniper Rifles

Snipers are long-range weapons designed for precision and high damage per shot.

  • Longbow DMR - A semi-automatic sniper with moderate fire rate. Good for long-range poking.
  • Charge Rifle - A hitscan energy sniper that fires a beam. Great for long-range precision.
  • Sentinel - A bolt-action sniper with high damage per shot. Ideal for long-range sniping.
  • Kraber .50-Cal (Care Package) - A powerful bolt-action sniper that can one-shot headshot. Best for long-range eliminations.

Shotguns

Shotguns are devastating at close range, offering high burst damage.

  • Mastiff - A semi-automatic shotgun with horizontal spread. Great for close-range fights.
  • Peacekeeper - A pump-action shotgun with high damage per shot. Best for close-range precision shots.
  • EVA-8 Auto - A fully automatic shotgun with fast fire rate. Good for close-range spam.
  • Mozambique (Shotgun Pistol) - A shotgun pistol with moderate damage. Best for early-game backup.

Pistols

Pistols are lightweight weapons ideal for early-game or as backups.

  • RE-45 Auto - A fully automatic pistol with high fire rate. Great for early-game or close-range backup.
  • P2020 - A semi-automatic pistol with moderate damage. Good for early-game or finishing weakened enemies.
  • Wingman - A heavy-hitting revolver with high damage per shot. Ideal for mid-range precision shots.

Marksman Weapons

Marksman weapons are versatile, offering a balance between snipers and assault rifles.

  • G7 Scout - A semi-automatic rifle with moderate damage. Great for mid-to-long range poking.
  • 30-30 Repeater - A lever-action rifle with high damage per shot. Good for mid-range precision shots.
  • Triple Take - A burst-fire energy rifle with spread shots. Ideal for mid-to-long range pressure.

Care Package Weapons

These are exclusive powerful weapons only found in Care Packages.

  • Kraber .50-Cal - A one-shot sniper rifle. Best for long-range eliminations.
  • Bocek Compound Bow - A silent, high-damage bow with chargeable shots. Great for mid-range stealth attacks.

Melee Weapons

Heirloom Melee Weapons are cosmetic melee weapons with unique animations. They are fast and silent, ideal for finishing moves or style points.

Maps

Storm Point

Storm Point is a tropical island map with dense jungles, high cliffs, and open beaches. It’s the largest map in Apex Legends, offering a mix of close-quarters combat and long-range engagements.

Strategies:

  • Early Game: Land in high-tier loot zones like Cenote Cave or The Mill for quick gear, but be prepared for early fights.
  • Mid Game: Use the map’s verticality to your advantage. Position yourself on high ground near choke points like The Wall or Command Center.
  • Late Game: Rotate early to avoid being caught in the storm. Use the map’s natural cover (trees, rocks) to avoid long-range snipers.

Pros:

  • Variety of Terrain: Offers a mix of open areas and tight spaces, catering to all playstyles.
  • PvE Elements: Wildlife (Prowlers and Spiders) can provide loot and shields, but be cautious as they can also reveal your position.
  • Verticality: High cliffs and ziplines allow for creative rotations and escapes.

Cons:

  • Large Size: Rotations can be slow without proper movement abilities or vehicles.
  • Third-Party Potential: Open areas make it easy for teams to third-party fights.
  • Wildlife: PvE elements can be a double-edged sword, as they can distract or damage you during fights.

World’s Edge

World’s Edge is a volcanic map with a mix of urban areas, icy tundras, and lava fields. It’s known for its distinct POIs and vertical gameplay.

Strategies:

  • Early Game: Land in hot zones like Fragment East or The Tree for quick action and loot, but expect heavy competition.
  • Mid Game: Control high-ground areas like Skyhook or Lava Siphon to dominate rotations.
  • Late Game: Use the map’s natural choke points (e.g., The Geyser or Lava Fissure) to trap enemies and secure wins.

Pros:

  • Distinct POIs: Each area feels unique, offering diverse combat scenarios.
  • Verticality: Multi-level buildings and ziplines allow for creative plays.
  • Rotations: Train tracks and geysers provide quick rotation options.

Cons:

  • Third-Party Central: Popular areas like Fragment attract constant third-parties.
  • Open Areas: Lava fields and icy tundras leave you exposed to long-range attacks.
  • RNG Loot: Some areas have inconsistent loot distribution, making early-game risky.

Olympus

Olympus is a floating city with a futuristic design, featuring open spaces, lush gardens, and high-tech structures. It’s a medium-sized map with a focus on mobility.

Strategies:

  • Early Game: Land in high-tier loot zones like Estates or Gardens for quick gear, but be ready for early fights.
  • Mid Game: Use the map’s vehicles (Tridents) for quick rotations and to escape third-parties.
  • Late Game: Control central areas like Phase Runner or Bonsai Plaza to dominate the final circles.

Pros:

  • Mobility: Tridents and Phase Runner portals make rotations fast and fun.
  • Aesthetic: The map’s bright, futuristic design is visually appealing.
  • Balanced Combat: Offers a mix of close-quarters and long-range engagements.

Cons:

  • Open Spaces: Large open areas can leave you exposed to snipers.
  • Third-Party Potential: High mobility means teams can quickly third-party fights.
  • RNG Loot: Some areas lack consistent loot, making early-game risky.

Broken Moon

Broken Moon is a lunar map with a mix of industrial zones, open plains, and futuristic structures. It’s a medium-sized map with a focus on verticality and unique rotation mechanics.

Strategies:

  • Early Game: Land in high-tier loot zones like The Core or Production Yard for quick gear, but expect early fights.
  • Mid Game: Use the map’s ziplines and gravity cannons for quick rotations and to control high ground.
  • Late Game: Focus on central areas like Breaker Wharf or The Divide to dominate the final circles.

Pros:

  • Unique Rotations: Gravity cannons and ziplines provide fast and creative rotation options.
  • Verticality: Multi-level structures and cliffs allow for high-ground control.
  • Balanced Combat: Offers a mix of close-quarters and long-range engagements.

Cons:

  • Third-Party Potential: High mobility means teams can quickly third-party fights.
  • Open Areas: Some areas lack cover, leaving you exposed to long-range attacks.
  • RNG Loot: Loot distribution can be inconsistent in certain areas.

E-District

E-District is a high-tier loot zone located in the northeastern part of Broken Moon. It’s a futuristic, industrial area with a mix of open courtyards, multi-level buildings, and tight corridors. The POI is designed for close-quarters combat and vertical gameplay, making it a hotspot for aggressive players. It’s also a central location on the map, making it a key rotation point for squads.

Strategies:

  • Early Game: Land on rooftops or inside the central building for quick loot. Be ready for immediate fights. Prioritize shotguns or SMGs for close-quarters combat.
  • Mid Game: Control high ground (e.g., central building) for tactical advantage. Use ziplines and gravity cannons for quick rotations to nearby POIs like The Core or Production Yard.
  • Late Game: Hold multi-level buildings for defensive positions. Use grenades to flush out enemies and watch for zipline pushes.

Pros:

  • High-Quality Loot: One of the best places to gear up quickly on Broken Moon.
  • Vertical Gameplay: Multi-level buildings and ziplines enable creative plays and high-ground control.
  • Central Location: Easy rotations to other POIs due to its position on the map.

Cons:

  • Hot Drop: Popular landing spot means heavy competition and early fights.
  • Open Spaces: Courtyards between buildings offer little cover, leaving you exposed to snipers.
  • Third-Party Potential: Central location makes it easy for squads from nearby POIs to third-party fights.

General Tips for All Maps

  • Rotate Early: Always plan your rotations ahead of time to avoid being caught in the storm.
  • Control High Ground: High ground offers better visibility and cover, making it easier to win fights.
  • Avoid Hot Drops (If You’re New): If you’re still learning, avoid high-traffic areas and focus on surviving to the late game.
  • Use Map-Specific Mechanics: Each map has unique features (e.g., Tridents on Olympus, gravity cannons on Broken Moon). Learn to use them to your advantage.
  • Watch for Third-Parties: Always be aware of your surroundings, as third-parties are common on all maps.

Tips and Tricks for Winning the Game

  • Stick with Your Team: Apex is a team-based game. Stick close to your squad to maximize your chances of survival. Use pings to communicate effectively if you’re not using voice chat.
  • Master Movement: Learn advanced movement techniques like slide-jumping, wall-bouncing, and zipline dancing to outmaneuver enemies. Always be moving during fights to avoid being an easy target.
  • Loot Efficiently: Prioritize shields, weapons, and ammo early game. Don’t spend too much time looting—stay aware of your surroundings. Share loot with your team, especially if they’re under-geared.
  • Play the Ring: Always be aware of the ring’s location and plan your rotations early. Use the ring to your advantage by positioning yourself to trap enemies outside it.
  • Third-Party Wisely: Listen for gunfire and use it to locate fights. Third-party when the enemy teams are weak, but be cautious of getting third-partied yourself.
  • Know When to Fight and When to Run: If you’re outnumbered or outgunned, disengage and regroup with your team. Use abilities like Wraith’s Into the Void or Octane’s Stim to escape sticky situations.

Weapon Tips

  • Close-Range: Use shotguns (Peacekeeper, Mastiff) or SMGs (R-99, CAR) for close-quarters combat. Hip-fire is highly effective in close-range fights.
  • Mid-Range: Assault rifles (R-301, Flatline) and marksman weapons (G7 Scout, 30-30 Repeater) are ideal for mid-range engagements. Tap-fire or use burst mode to control recoil.
  • Long-Range: Snipers (Charge Rifle, Sentinel) are perfect for picking off enemies from a distance. Use cover and high ground to avoid return fire.

Advanced Tips

  • High Ground Advantage: Always try to control high ground during fights. It gives you better visibility and makes it harder for enemies to hit you.
  • Grenades Are Your Friend: Use grenades to flush out enemies hiding behind cover or to block off choke points.
  • Shield Swapping: In the middle of a fight, quickly swap your broken shield for a fresh one from a death box. This can save your life.
  • Bait Enemies: Use decoys (Mirage) or fake rotations to bait enemies into unfavorable positions.
  • Practice in the Firing Range: Spend time in the Firing Range to practice recoil control, movement, and weapon swaps.

Movement

Movement is one of the most critical aspects of Apex Legends. Mastering advanced movement techniques can give you a significant edge in fights, help you escape dangerous situations, and outmaneuver your opponents. Below, I’ll break down essential movement tips and tricks, including basic and advanced techniques, to help you become a movement master.

Basic Movement Tips

  • Always Be Moving: Standing still makes you an easy target. Strafe (move side-to-side) while shooting to make it harder for enemies to hit you.
  • Crouch Spamming: Crouch repeatedly during fights to throw off your enemy’s aim. Combine this with strafing for maximum effectiveness.
  • Slide Often: Sliding is faster than running and makes you a harder target to hit. Use it to move downhill or to quickly reposition during fights.
  • Jumping: Jumping can help you avoid shots, but don’t overuse it. Predictable jump patterns make you an easy target.
  • Climbing: Use climbing to access high ground or escape enemies. Remember, you can climb most walls and structures.

Advanced Movement Techniques

  • Slide Jumping: Sprint, then crouch to slide. As you gain speed, jump at the end of the slide to maintain momentum. Use this to move faster across open areas or to quickly reposition during fights.
  • Wall Bouncing: Sprint toward a wall, jump, and then jump again as you make contact with the wall to bounce off at an angle. Use this to quickly change direction and confuse enemies or to access high ground.
  • Zipline Dancing: While on a zipline, jump and change direction mid-air to avoid being an easy target. Use this when rotating across ziplines in high-traffic areas or to escape enemies chasing you on a zipline.
  • Tap-Strafing (PC Only): Use a combination of air strafing and scroll wheel inputs to make sharp turns mid-air. Use this to make unpredictable movements during fights or to quickly change direction while sliding or jumping.
  • Bunny Hopping (B-Hopping): Jump and crouch repeatedly while moving to maintain momentum after sliding. Use this to move quickly while healing or to maintain speed after sliding downhill.
  • Super Glide: At the end of a climb, press jump and crouch simultaneously to launch yourself forward with extra speed. Use this to quickly gain distance or reposition during fights or to escape enemies chasing you after climbing.
